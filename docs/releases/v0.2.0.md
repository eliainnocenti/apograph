# Apograph v0.2.0 — two PoliTo beta templates

This prerelease expands the tested collection to two unofficial Politecnico di
Torino templates and introduces the first source-distributed Apograph command-
line client. The released template ZIPs remain the canonical user products.

## Included templates

- `presentation-beamer-polito-latex`: the presentation starter and one-file
  teaching showcase based on Mattia Ippoliti's adaptation, with complete
  attribution and compiling legal-safe branding fallbacks.
- `thesis-polito-latex`: a faithful packaging of Luigi De Russis's Bachelor's
  and Master's thesis template, with isolated configuration, modular content,
  and a compiling same-footprint logo fallback.

Both templates vendor their required dependencies inside the ZIP and compile
without access to the Apograph repository. A packed ZIP of the promoted thesis
source revision was tested on Overleaf with pdfLaTeX. CI compiles the source and
packed artifacts with TeX Live 2026; both exact CI-built release ZIPs are checked
on Overleaf before the protected publication tag is created.

## Experimental command-line client

The source tag includes the first dependency-free Python `apograph` client. It
can list and inspect the templates in a published release, download an exact
template ZIP, verify its release-index SHA-256 digest, and extract it without
overwriting an existing destination. PyPI publication is intentionally deferred
until the source-distributed interface has been exercised.

## Known limitations

- These templates are unofficial and are not endorsed by Politecnico di Torino.
- No PoliTo logo or branded background is redistributed. Users may add marks
  they are independently authorized to use at the documented paths.
- The command-line interface is an early beta and may change before its first
  package-index release.
- Public theme commands and layout details may change before a stable release.

Report reproducible problems through [Apograph Issues](https://github.com/eliainnocenti/apograph/issues).
Include the template ID, release version, compiler/environment, and the smallest
source that demonstrates the problem.
